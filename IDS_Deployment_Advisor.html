📐 Estimated Label-Conflict Floor (ELCF)
If your dataset's clean-data FNR approaches the theoretical Label-Conflict Floor (estimated by computing feature-hash conflicts in your NetFlow data), further model optimisation yields diminishing returns. In NF-UNSW-NB15-v3, 642 hash conflicts produced an ELCF of ~0.14% FNR. Consider improving data quality rather than model architecture when operating near this floor.
⚠ Scope of this recommendation: This tool is a proof-of-concept research prototype validated on NF-UNSW-NB15-v3 (17.5:1 imbalance) and NF-CICIDS2018-v3 (6.7:1 imbalance). Failure Mode Types 2 and 3 are based on single-seed reference runs and should be treated as empirically motivated hypotheses. Independent multi-seed validation on your target dataset is required before finalising deployment decisions.

Failure Mode Taxonomy
Five mechanistically distinct failure behaviours identified across three NF-* benchmark datasets. Click any card to expand the full evidence and deployment rule.
TYPE 1
Gradual Attenuation
Baseline CNN-LSTM · DAE · SCE/CW on DS3
MechanismNoise corrupts decision boundary proportionally; no discrete failure threshold; degradation monotonic with contamination level.
Risk levelLOW
Deployment ruleMonitor FNR trend over time; no pre-deployment exclusion required.
Evidence: Baseline DS1 — all p>0.43 across N=5 seeds, 0–30% noise. DAE DS1 — mean F1 within 0.0005 of Baseline at every level.
TYPE 2
Gradient Inversion
Class-weight adjustment · DS1 (17.5:1)
MechanismInverse-frequency amplification applies equally to genuine and noise-corrupted minority labels, inverting gradient direction. AUC remains intact throughout collapse.
Risk levelCRITICAL
Key findingAUC-F1 divergence: AUC >0.999 while F1 collapses — invisible to AUC-only monitoring dashboards.
Deployment ruleHigh risk when benign:attack >10:1. Dual-metric monitoring (FNR + F1 + AUC) mandatory.
Evidence: CW DS1 (single-seed reference) — F1=0.9958 at 0% noise; F1=0.1030 from 10% noise. Gradient inversion threshold ~5.4% estimated.
TYPE 3a
Structural Incompatibility
SCE Loss · DS1 (17.5:1) · Single-seed exploratory
MechanismRCE term penalises confident majority predictions. Under severe imbalance, confident benign prediction is statistically optimal — not pathological. RCE amplifies the dominant gradient.
Risk levelCRITICAL (exploratory)
Key findingF1=0.8474 (FNR=25.6%) at 0% noise; collapse (F1=0.0000) at 5%, 10%, 20%, 30%; partial bistable recovery at 15%.
Deployment ruleAvoid SCE when benign:attack substantially >10:1. Failure unlikely to be resolved by simple parameter tuning without altering loss-design assumptions.
Evidence: SCE DS1 (seed=42 reference run only). alpha=0.01, beta=0.1. Multi-seed verification not conducted — treat as hypothesis.
TYPE 3b
Boundary Instability
SCE Loss · DS2 (6.72:1) · N=5 verified
MechanismSame RCE mechanism at moderate imbalance. Failure is probabilistic and partial — FNR ~41–42% without complete collapse. Highly seed-dependent.
Risk levelHIGH — operationally dangerous
Key findingMore dangerous than Type 3a: single-run F1 looks acceptable (~0.967) while FNR ranges 19–100% across seeds. Evades single-metric monitoring.
Deployment rulePer-seed FNR tracking mandatory. Never rely on single training run for SCE at moderate imbalance.
Evidence: SCE DS2 — N=5 seeds × 6 noise levels = 30 runs. FNR 19–100% across runs. Mean FNR ~41.7% (degenerate state).
TYPE 4
Bottleneck Limitation
DAE Preprocessing · DS2 vs DS1
MechanismFixed-capacity DAE encoder (64-dim bottleneck) discards discriminatively relevant variance when attack category diversity exceeds representation capacity.
Risk levelLOW-MODERATE
Key findingDAE 0.001–0.002 below Baseline on DS2 (15 attack categories) vs DS1 (9 categories). Statistically significant at p<0.02 (N=5).
Deployment ruleScale bottleneck dimensionality proportionally to attack category count. DAE still preferred over SCE/CW under severe imbalance.
Evidence: DAE DS2 N=5 — consistently 0.001–0.002 below Baseline. DAE 107,889 params; bottleneck input(49)→256→128→64→128→256→49.

Key findings
🎯
Strategy effectiveness is context-dependent, not intrinsic
Class-weight adjustment is simultaneously the most dangerous strategy (DS1, gradient inversion) and the best mitigation strategy (DS3, attack-majority). Identical code, opposite outcomes — determined entirely by class imbalance direction.
Supported by DS1 vs DS3 cross-dataset comparison
📐
Estimated Label-Conflict Floor (ELCF) — original contribution
642 feature-hash conflicts in DS1 establish a theoretical minimum FNR of ~0.14%. The CNN-LSTM Baseline achieved FNR=0.21% on clean data — within 0.07pp of this floor. Contamination-induced degradation cannot push FNR below the ELCF regardless of model architecture.
DS1: 642/127,693 attack records = ~0.14% ELCF
⚠️
AUC-F1 divergence makes Class-weight collapse invisible
During Type 2 failure, AUC-ROC remains >0.999 while F1 collapses to ~0.10. A SOC monitoring only AUC would see no signal of failure. Dual-metric monitoring (FNR + F1 alongside AUC) is a necessary safeguard.
DS1 Class-weight single-seed reference: AUC=0.999, F1=0.1030 at 10%+ noise
🔬
Imbalance ratio is a causal contributor — controlled study evidence
Reducing DS2's ratio from 6.72:1 to exact 1:1 balance (holding all other factors fixed) fully stabilised SCE Type 3b instability. The same manipulation on DS1 produced chaotic instability — indicating an additional unidentified structural factor beyond binary ratio.
DS2 1:1: F1=0.960, std<0.01 (N=4). DS1 1:1: F1 range 0.002–0.999 (N=4)
⚠ Research scope: SCE and class-weight experiments conducted as single-seed reference runs on DS1. Types 2 and 3a should be treated as empirically motivated hypotheses requiring multi-seed validation. Type 3b (DS2 SCE) is multi-seed verified (N=5, 30 runs). Statistical power: N=5 → ~25% power at d=0.5; N=27 required for 80% power.
